# How the project operates

The Longhand Archive holds itself to eight operating principles. They are not aspirations. They are the rules the project applies.

**Archival integrity over convenience.** The archive is the authoritative record. Everything built on top of it — databases, websites, APIs — is a derived view, rebuildable from the archive. When integrity and convenience conflict, integrity wins.

**Preservation serves intelligence.** The archive exists to be understood, not just kept. Preservation without analysis is storage. Analysis without preservation is opinion.

**Deliberate pace.** Structure is chosen before code is written. Decisions are recorded before they are implemented. The project optimises for the quality of what endures, not the speed at which things appear.

**Built in public.** Decisions, reasoning, and reversals are visible. The messiness is part of the value. The polish is not the point.

**The process is the product.** One person, building a substantive system using AI-assisted tools, documenting the architecture, the decisions, and the working method as they happen. The reasoning is as reusable as the code.

**Independent and non-endorsed.** The project is not affiliated with or endorsed by any UK government department or any other organisation. It uses public data under the Open Government Licence. Its posture is observational, not adversarial.

**Privacy by design.** Personal data is minimised at the earliest possible boundary. The public surface never reveals more than its purpose requires.

**Generalisation by evidence.** Abstractions are extracted from at least two concrete examples, not guessed up front. The first instance of anything is allowed to be specific. Shared patterns are proven, not assumed.

These principles are maintained in the [governance repository](https://github.com/longhandarchive/governance).
